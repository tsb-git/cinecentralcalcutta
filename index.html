Mongolian Film Festival in Kolkata

We are privileged to hold a film festival in the big screen after a longtime. We screened three contemporary Mongolian films in the festival.

A colourful inaugural ceremony was held on Friday,1 April 22 at Nandan. Renowned Cine action Arjun Chakraborty inagurated the festival, the Ambassador of Mongolia Mr Gambold Dambajav was present as Chief Guest. Other speakers were Mr GG Dalmia, Hony. Consul of Mongolia and Mr Parimal Mukherjee, General Secretary. For two days the packed auditorium witnessed movies from a distant country, which produces films in limited number, but make very interesting and powerful Cinema.

We are grateful to the Embassy of Mongolia in New Delhi and the Consulate Office in Kolkata for helping us to organise this festival.



Pittu is no more

With the death of Animesh Mukherjee, popularity known to all as Pittu, we lost a shining jewel of our society. He died on Sunday, after a brief illness. For over Fifty years he devotedly worked to organise various programmes. He held many posts like Asst. Secretary, Joint Secretary and Vice President.

Always smiling, soft voice, tall, handsome Pittu was a familiar face in the Bengali film atena. He was also involved in the film production. He produced some interesting Documentary and TV serial in Doordarshan. He was also involved in the production of famous Bengali news programme Khas Khabar.

We convey our heartfelt condolences to the bereaved family members. Our tearful farewell homage to dear friend





Cine Central, Calcutta presents A Day in the Life of Jamaluddin

A documentary (26 minutes / Digital format )

Director : Sekhar Banerjee

Assistant Director : Raka Banerjee

Producer : Ordinary Films

It is a tale of poor and unlettered Jamaluddin who spent nearly half a century selling used vinyl records of almost all genres of Indian and international music from his roadside shack at Lenin Sarani, Kolkata, India. This documentary seeks to capture the pluralistic world of a man who grew up and grew old with vinyl records. Jamaluddin, a devout muslim and a responsible father of a family of four daughters and two sons, has been selling second-hand records since the age of 14. Growing up in the musically turbulent sixties and the seventies, he is a living chronicler of the lost world of vinyl records and an eye witness to a bygone era.  This documentary seeks to portray the inseparable history of Jamaluddin and vinyl records which transcends communities, languages and religions in the course of a day. It is a take on the changing pattern of music business, the mood of a busy city of nearly ten million people and the nostalgia of vinyl records. It is also a musical journey with Jamaluddin across cultures and continents as well. P.S. The Director returned to Jamaluddins shop after two months to find that the shop which belonged to Jamaluddin had vanished like the era of vinyl records. A multistoried building is coming up in its place.
